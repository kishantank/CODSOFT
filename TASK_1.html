Viswanathan Anand

Viswanathan Anand, also known as "Vishy," is one of the greatest chess players in the world. He became India’s first Grandmaster in 1988 and has inspired generations with his intelligence, humility, and dedication. Known for his rapid playing style and strategic depth, he has been a global ambassador of Indian chess.

Key Achievements

  • First Indian to become a Grandmaster – 1988
  • Five-time World Chess Champion (2000, 2007, 2008, 2010, 2012)
  • Won the FIDE World Rapid Championship (2003, 2017)
  • First Asian to win the World Chess Championship
  • Defeated legends like Garry Kasparov, Vladimir Kramnik, and Magnus Carlsen
  • Awarded Padma Shri (1987), Padma Bhushan (2000), and Padma Vibhushan (2007)
  • Played a major role in popularizing chess in India

Interesting Facts

  • ♟️ Known as the “Lightning Kid” for his rapid moves in youth tournaments
  • ♟️ Speaks fluent Spanish from living in Spain during his early chess career
  • ♟️ Maintains a calm and composed demeanor even under pressure
  • ♟️ Supports chess education programs across India
  • ♟️ Wrote a best-selling book titled "Mind Master: Winning Lessons from a Champion's Life"
